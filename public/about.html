About Five in a Row

Five in a Row (also known as Gomoku, Gobang, or Wuziqi) is a classic abstract strategy game played on a Go board. Two players take turns placing stones of their own color on the intersections of a 15×15 grid. The first player to align five stones in a continuous row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — wins the game.

How to play

  • You play as White. The computer plays as Black.
  • Click any empty intersection to place a stone.
  • The first side to align five of their own stones in a row wins.
  • Whoever moves first is decided randomly at the start of each match.
  • You will see an alert message at the end of the game indicating whether you won or lost.

Behind the game

This implementation is based on a tiny jQuery plugin by Zebra. The entire game logic — board state, AI move generation, win detection — fits in roughly 1.7 KB of minified JavaScript (~330 lines unminified). The AI evaluates each empty cell against simple heuristics for offense and defense and picks the highest-scoring move.

The page itself uses no build tools, no frameworks, and no back end. The board is rendered as a single HTML

styled with plain CSS. The game runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server, and no account is required.

Why we built this

We love small, self-contained programs that do one thing well. Five in a Row is a public good: a free, playable classic that runs on any device with a web browser, including older hardware. It exists so anyone, anywhere, can take a quick break and play a fair match of Gomoku against a competent opponent in under thirty seconds.
